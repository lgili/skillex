# Style and Language

Write in technical English that is easy to scan.

## Sentence Rules

- Prefer short declarative sentences.
- Use active voice when the actor matters.
- Use passive voice only when the process matters more than the actor.
- Remove filler such as "it should be noted that", "it is important to mention", and "it can be seen that".
- Avoid repeating the same claim in different words.

## Preferred Style

Weak:

`The results appear to show that the converter is performing in a reasonably stable manner.`

Stronger:

`The converter remained stable across the tested load range.`

Weak:

`A significant increase in temperature was observed during operation.`

Stronger:

`Case temperature increased from 48 C to 72 C during the load step.`

## Paragraph Rules

- Keep paragraphs short.
- One paragraph should usually support one claim.
- If a paragraph exceeds roughly six lines, check whether a table or figure should replace part of it.
- If a paragraph contains many numbers, convert them into a table.

## Word Choice

Prefer:

- measured
- estimated
- derived
- validated
- limited
- unstable
- within tolerance
- outside specification

Avoid unsupported words:

- good
- bad
- nice
- robust
- efficient
- improved
- optimized

Only use these when the report explains how they were judged.

## Tone

- Technical, calm, direct
- No marketing language
- No dramatic phrasing
- No unnecessary adjectives
- No rhetorical questions

## Rewrite Heuristic

When editing a weak paragraph:

1. Find the main claim.
2. Keep the evidence.
3. Delete framing language.
4. Replace vague words with measured or bounded statements.
5. Check whether the claim belongs in a figure caption, table, or equation instead.
